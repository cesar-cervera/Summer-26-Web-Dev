Predictive Budgeting & Overdraft Risk Modeling

Abstract

Financial stress is a major challenge for college students and is closely linked to academic outcomes and mental well-being. One of the most harmful consequences of financial instability is overdrafting, which disproportionately affects young adults with limited income stability and financial literacy. This project investigates whether early financial behavior can be used to predict future overdraft risk using a longitudinal dataset of 1,360 individuals spanning 24 months. We evaluate Logistic Regression, Random Forest, and Bidirectional LSTM models and find that a well-regularized Logistic Regression model outperforms more complex temporal approaches, achieving an F1-score of 94.12% and a ROC-AUC of 0.9976.

  • Constructed and cleaned a longitudinal financial dataset spanning 24 months
  • Engineered statistical features for early overdraft risk prediction
  • Conducted model evaluation and analyzed comparative performance results

Key result: Logistic Regression achieved F1 = 0.94 and ROC-AUC = 0.9976

Evaluating Retrieval-Augmented Generation for Fake News Detection

Abstract

With the growing prevalence of AI-generated content and bias in news reporting, fake news has become increasingly harmful to public discourse. This project investigates whether RAG improves large language model performance on fake news detection tasks using the Snopes benchmark dataset and GPT-4o. Results reveal a tradeoff: while RAG significantly improves validation of true claims, performance on false claims degrades due to confirmation bias introduced by search results.

  • Prepared and cleaned the Snopes benchmark dataset for evaluation
  • Conducted comparative analysis of zero-shot vs RAG-enhanced model outputs
  • Analyzed performance tradeoffs and identified confirmation bias effects

Key finding: RAG improves validation of true claims but degrades fake-claim detection due to retrieval bias
