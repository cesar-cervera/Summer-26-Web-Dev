Youth Vaping in the Digital Age: A Systematic Review of Technology-Based Interventions

Milkowski*, Olesk*, Martel Asfura, Barco, Cervera, Wachira, Sisk, Balke, Purushotham, Williams, Badillo-Urquiola

Abstract

Youth vaping has emerged as a significant public health concern, with rising prevalence among adolescents and young adults. This paper presents a systematic review of peer-reviewed literature examining technology-based interventions for youth vaping prevention and cessation. We analyze intervention modalities including mobile applications, social media campaigns, text-messaging programs, and immersive technologies, identifying patterns in design approaches, engagement strategies, and evaluation methods. We identify gaps in youth-centered design and evaluation rigor, and outline directions for future research that integrate principles from human-computer interaction and public health.

Ethical for Whom? Examining Cross-Cultural Perspectives on AI Values

Systematic Literature Review

Abstract

This work examines how values associated with responsible and ethical AI are articulated across diverse cultural and geographic contexts, with a particular focus on communities in the Global Majority. Through a systematic review of peer-reviewed literature, we analyze how AI values are framed, operationalized, and prioritized across different cultural settings. Our findings reveal recurring tensions between universalist ethical frameworks and community-centered values. This work argues for participatory and pluralistic approaches to responsible AI that more directly incorporate the perspectives and lived experiences of underrepresented communities.
